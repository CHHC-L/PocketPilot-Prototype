Usability testing script · a. Greeting & introduction

What the facilitator says first

“Thanks for helping us out today. Before we start, a few things I want to be clear about:

• We're testing the app, not you — there are no wrong moves, and anything you find confusing is useful feedback for us.
• You can stop at any time, for any reason, and you don't have to explain why.
• Please think out loud as you go — tell me what you're looking at, what you expect to happen, and where you get stuck. Ask questions freely, though I may not answer some so I can see what you'd do on your own.
• We've already created a test account and signed you in, with a sample file loaded, so you can focus on the tasks.

I'll give you a few short tasks. Ready when you are.”

08
Usability testing script · b. Research questions

What the test tries to answer

Derived from our value proposition. We never ask testers these directly — we infer answers from how they perform the tasks.
  • RQ1 — Task expression: Can a first-time user state a real task in plain language and trust PocketPilot to carry it out?
  • RQ2 — Privacy transparency: Do users understand what stays on their phone versus what the cloud sees, and does that build trust?
  • RQ3 — Verifiability: Can users confirm for themselves that sensitive details (a file, a contact) were only ever anonymous labels to the cloud?
  • RQ4 — Control: Do users feel in control at the confirmation step, and can they stop or edit before anything is sent?
  • RQ5 — Onboarding friction: Can users reach and complete core value in anonymous mode before any account or device pairing?

09
Usability testing script · c. Tasks per research question

Tasks (given without telling users how)

  • T1 (RQ1): “You have a PDF and want the app to summarise it and prepare a reply email to your groupmate. Get PocketPilot to do it.”
  • T2 (RQ2): “Before the assistant does anything, find out what information about you leaves your phone, and what the cloud can see.”
  • T3 (RQ3): “After the task finishes, check whether your groupmate's real details were ever sent to the cloud.”
  • T4 (RQ4): “The reply isn't quite right — change it before it's sent.”
  • T5 (RQ5): “You just installed PocketPilot and haven't set anything up. Try what it can do before creating an account or connecting a device.”
Tasks assume the tester is already signed in to a prepared test account.

11
Usability testing script · e. Pre- & post-test questionnaires

Screening & de-brief

Pre-test — classification only (no tutorial)

  • Age range and smartphone OS you use most.
  • How often do you use voice / AI assistants (Siri, ChatGPT, …)?
  • Have you ever done a multi-app chore by hand (e.g. read a file, then email someone about it)?
  • How concerned are you about apps sending your data to the cloud? (1–5)
  • Do you have a technical / CS background? (y/n)

Post-test — de-brief

  • I understood what left my phone and what stayed on it. (1–5)
  • I trusted PocketPilot to carry out the task. (1–5)
  • The confirmation step made me feel in control. (1–5)
  • What, if anything, was confusing or unexpected? (open)
  • Would you use this for real tasks? Why / why not? (open)
